Her Story


Demi Diaz is a go-getter, born and bred in New York City.

From a young age, she has always demonstrated an entrepreneurial spirit. When she was the tender age of 5 years old, her neighbors would come over to get pampered in her imaginary beauty business with her imaginary employees. Hair, nails, or make up? Demi's boutique had you covered! As she grew older, she would join her family at the social club, Centro Cultural Deportivo Dominicano, and as a passtime would push down soap and hand out paper towels in the women's bathroom for tips. While other kids spent their summers at camp, watching TV or playing video games, Demi was at her father's softball games in Randall's Island selling homemade treats and water. Thank you Betty Crocker!

All of this before she received her legal working papers at the age of 14.

Her hustler spirit followed her throughout her educational career. At The Beacon School, she was the President of the Student Movement Against Cancer, an organization that partnered with The American Cancer Society and St. Jude Children's Hospital to raise awareness and funds for cancer research and treatment. She led meetings, organized fundraisers, and managed logistics leading up to their annual Relay For Life event where the school raised over $5,000 in donations. She continued to refine her creative marketing skills at Fordham University as the Public Relations Coordinator for the socio-cultural club, El Grito de Lares. There, she dabbled into planning and promotions curating events at the Rose Hill campus. Her drive and determination to succeed has since then afforded her incredible opportunities with companies like Sprinkles Cupcakes, Def Jam Records, Roc Nation, Tidal, Chase, Coca-Cola, and SONY. Every opportunity has helped her refine her professional skills and fueled her entrepreneurial drive.

When she's not working as a Performance Marketing Coordinator or Brand Representative, she is cultivating her own - continuing to share the blueprint on the hustle.
