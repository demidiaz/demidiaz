My Definition of Success


Have you ever asked yourself, what your “why?” is? Why do you work as hard as you do? Why is succeeding important to you? A friend recently asked me, "What do you want out of life?" My immediate answer was: I want to be happy. I want to be financially secure. I want to live comfortably. I want my parents to be set when they retire. I want to continue being resourceful and provide for those around me.

He proceeded to ask me, “Ok, but, how will you define that? How will you measure your success?"

It really made me think. He's right. When will I have that "momma, I made it!" moment?

One thing you'll learn about me is that I love making lists (to do lists, bucket lists, itinerary lists, every-ting!)

Here are my personal definitions of success. As ridiculous as some of these may sound, these are things that I am genuinely looking forward to.

I have purposely started each sentence with "I will" to affirm that I am determined to do it.

  1. I will have a globally-recognized brand.
  2. I will be featured in Forbes 30 Under 30 list.
  3. I will invest in young entrepreneurs.
  4. I will create my own line of calendars and planners.
  5. I will work on a project to spread love and positivity with one of my favorite artists, Miguel.
  6. I will partner with Apple Music to create my own radio show.
  7. I will increase my credit score to 760.
  8. I will own a brownstone in Harlem.
  9. I will provide for my parents when they retire.
  10. I will continue networking and being resourceful for as long as I live because, "your network is your net worth."

What's on your list?
